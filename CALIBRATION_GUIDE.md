# Calibration Guide for KTY 84/130 Temperature Sensor

## Introduction
This guide provides step-by-step instructions for calibrating the KTY 84/130 temperature sensor. Follow the steps carefully to ensure accurate temperature measurements.

## Reference Points
Before proceeding with the calibration, gather the following reference points:

1. **Ice Water**: Prepare a mixture of ice and water to achieve a temperature of 0°C.
2. **Boiling Water**: Prepare boiling water to achieve a temperature of 100°C.
3. **Room Temperature**: Measure the temperature in a controlled environment, typically around 20°C.

## Calibration Steps
### 1. Setup
- Ensure that the KTY 84/130 sensor is properly connected to the measurement device.
- Use a multimeter to measure resistance.

### 2. Calibration at 0°C (Ice Water)
1. Immerse the KTY 84/130 sensor in the ice water mixture.
2. Wait for at least 5 minutes to ensure temperature stabilization.
3. Measure the resistance using the multimeter.
4. Record the resistance value. It should be approximately 10.3 kΩ at 0°C.

### 3. Calibration at 20°C (Room Temperature)
1. Place the sensor in a controlled environment with a stable room temperature.
2. After adequate stabilization, measure the resistance again.
3. Record the resistance value. It should be around 12.5 kΩ at 20°C.

### 4. Calibration at 100°C (Boiling Water)
1. Immerse the sensor in boiling water (ensure it is not in direct contact with the pot). 
2. After stabilization, measure the resistance.
3. Record the resistance value. It should be approximately 5.1 kΩ at 100°C.

## Calculation Methods
- The calibration curve can be plotted using the resistance values at the three reference points.
- Use the formula for temperature in °C from the resistance:
  
  \[ T(°C) = \frac{R - R_0}{\alpha} + T_0 \]
  
  Where:  
  - T is the temperature in °C  
  - R is the measured resistance  
  - R_0 is the resistance at 0°C  
  - \( \alpha \) is the temperature coefficient  
  - T_0 is the reference temperature

### Conclusion
Follow these calibration steps regularly to ensure the accurate functioning of the KTY 84/130 temperature sensor. Proper calibration will enhance the reliability of temperature measurements in various applications.
